The Core Framework: Systems & Interactions

In dynamics, we transition from describing how objects move to investigating why they change their state of motion. The foundational step in any complex physics problem is isolating our system of interest and analyzing its external constraints.

Newton's Second Law (Vector Notation):
ΣF = m * a

When modeling systems computationally, we treat complex structures as networks of simpler particles interacting via internal forces, while external forces dictate the system's net center-of-mass acceleration.
